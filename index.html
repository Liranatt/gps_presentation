Garmin Health Intelligence: Deterministic Math Meets Multi-Agent AI

An autonomous Data Engineering and AI platform designed to analyze my personal Garmin watch data. This system solves the LLM "hallucination" problem through a "Determinism before Semantics" architecture:
Deterministic Math Layer: A custom statistical engine (Python/Pandas) processes my raw biometric sensors before any AI involvement. It executes rigorous calculations—including Pearson correlations, AR(1) autoregressive models, Markov Chain state transitions, and statistical anomaly detection—to establish mathematical ground truth.
Multi-Agent System: A squad of 5 specialized AI agents (CrewAI) interprets these pre-validated results in parallel. From sleep architecture to training load optimization, they synthesize actionable insights grounded strictly in my specific physiological data, rather than generic training sets.
The result is an N-of-1 scientific instrument that understands my body better than any standard health app algorithm.

Deep Learning Research

Visual Localization: Direct Coordinate Regression via Deep Ensembles

A Deep Learning system that treats visual localization as a continuous regression problem rather than a discrete retrieval task.
Physics-Aware Optimization: Implemented a custom, numerically stable Haversine Loss function (PyTorch) to optimize for actual geodesic distance on Earth's surface, solving gradient explosion issues common in coordinate regression.
Architecture: An ensemble of EfficientNet-B0, ResNet18, and ConvNeXt backbones, utilizing Transfer Learning to extract spatial features robust to lighting and viewpoint changes.
Result: Achieved 5.24m mean error in a GPS-denied environment (BGU Campus), proving the viability of autonomous navigation using monocular vision alone.
